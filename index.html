Weeks 1–2

Call Listening Log

Shadow at least 5 calls during Weeks 1 and 2. Complete one log entry per call. Focus on tone, structure, how objections are handled, and how the SSA builds rapport with the student.

Ask your buddy or manager to sit with you for your first call shadows. Jot quick notes during the call, then complete this log right after while it's fresh.

Call recordings to listen to: Open call library
Reflection: After completing all 5 call logs
Weeks 1–2

Role Play Scenarios

Complete these role plays with your buddy or manager before going fully independent on calls. Repeat any scenario until you feel confident. Tick the box when you're happy with your performance.

It's fine to repeat a scenario more than once. The goal is confidence, not just completion. Your notes here will be useful to revisit as you start taking real calls.
Scenario 1
A student calls in saying they are thinking about withdrawing. They feel overwhelmed with study and work and have not engaged for 3 weeks.
Objective: Practice empathetic listening, identify the root concern and explore re-engagement options.
Scenario 2
You call a student to check in. They are short with you and say they are too busy and do not see the point of these calls.
Objective: Practice handling resistance and finding a reason to reconnect.
Scenario 3
A student tells you they are struggling academically and worried about failing their next assessment. They are distressed.
Objective: Practice active listening, provide appropriate support and referral pathways, and document the call correctly.
Scenario 4
You are following up on a re-enrolment for a student who has not responded to 3 previous contact attempts. You finally reach them.
Objective: Practice a confident re-enrolment conversation that leads to a commitment or a clear next step.
Scenario 5
A student is very engaged and mentions a friend who is also studying and struggling. They ask if you can help their friend.
Objective: Practice navigating an out-of-scope request professionally while maintaining the positive relationship.
Weeks 1–8

Weekly Goal Setting

Set 2–3 goals at the start of each week with your manager. Goals should be specific, achievable within the week, and linked to your development focus or probation objectives.

Weeks 1–8

Weekly Reflection Journal

Complete your reflection at the end of each week before your catch-up with your manager. This is your personal record, not a formal assessment — be honest with yourself.

Week 8

Self-Assessment

Complete this at the end of Week 8 before your Month 2 probation review. Rate yourself honestly — this is a conversation starter, not a test.

Rating scale:   1 = Not yet confident  ·  2 = Developing  ·  3 = Mostly there  ·  4 = Confident  ·  5 = Strong — could coach others
